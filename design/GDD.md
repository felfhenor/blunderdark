# Blunderdark

*A dungeon creation and management sim.*

# Mission Statement

Create an interesting fantasy dungeon with room placements and unique inhabitants, finding interesting synergies to make a functioning dungeon ecosystem where multiple valid strategies exist.

---

# Core Philosophy

**Anti-Optimization Design Pillars:**
1. **Dynamic Adjacency** - Room effects change based on neighbors, creating spatial puzzles
2. **Conditional Bonuses** - Inhabitants gain bonuses from specific contexts, not just room types
3. **Resource Tension** - Multiple currencies compete for investment, no single path dominates
4. **Temporal Variance** - Day/night cycles, seasons, and events shift optimal strategies
5. **Emergent Synergies** - Interactions between 3+ elements create unexpected combinations

---

# Game Features

* Create an underground dungeon across multiple floors with unique biomes
* Each room has tetromino-inspired shapes (cannot rotate)
* Connect rooms with hallways (upgradeable with features)
* **Adjacency matters**: Rooms affect neighbors with fear, inspiration, contamination, or resonance
* Assign workers with unique traits that activate conditionally
* Upgrade rooms through branching paths (mutually exclusive choices)
* Navigate day/night cycles that affect production and inhabitant behavior
* Manage floor depth bonuses and penalties
* Defend your dungeon with strategic trap and creature placement
* Fuse inhabitants to create hybrid specialists
* **Seasonal events** change resource values and spawn special visitors
* **Dungeon reputation** affects what creatures you can recruit

## Room Availability Balancing

### Purchase Cooldowns:

Some rooms have cooldown periods to prevent spam:

- **After building Soul Well:** 5-day cooldown before building another
- **After building Summoning Circle:** 7-day cooldown before building another
- **After building Ley Line Nexus:** 15-day cooldown (major construction)
- **After building Tier 3 room:** 3-day cooldown on all Tier 3 builds

### Floor Restrictions:

Certain rooms have floor placement restrictions:

- **Mushroom Grove:** Cannot be placed below Floor 5 (needs ambient light)
- **Underground Lake:** Can only be placed on floors with natural water (random, show indicator)
- **Ley Line Nexus:** Can only be placed on floors with ley line convergence (random spawn, marked on grid)
- **Geothermal rooms:** Floors 4+ only
- **Surface structures:** Floor 1 only

### Biome Restrictions:

Floors can have biomes that restrict room types:

- **Volcanic Biome:** Cannot build Underground Lake or Mushroom Grove
- **Flooded Biome:** Cannot build Soul Well or Torture Chamber (water ruins them)
- **Crystal Cave Biome:** Crystal Mines +50% effective, max 5 mines per floor
- **Corrupted Biome:** Cannot build Mushroom Grove or "pure" rooms

## Room Destruction & Rebuilding

### Voluntary Destruction:
- Player can demolish rooms (receive 50% refund)
- Takes 1 day to demolish
- Clears grid space
- Inhabitants must be reassigned first

### Invasion Damage:
- Sacked rooms lose 20-40% production until repaired
- Repair cost: 50% of original build cost
- Repair time: 2 days
- Heavily damaged rooms (80%+) must be rebuilt fully

### Room Upgrades Count Toward Limits:
- Upgraded Soul Well still counts as 1 Soul Well (doesn't break limit)
- Cannot demolish and rebuild to reset upgrades (upgrades persist on rebuild if within 7 days)

- **Season of Growth:** Food-based rooms cost -25%
- **Season of Darkness:** Dark/corruption rooms cost -25%, no moral penalties
- **Season of Harvest:** All rooms -10% cost
- **Season of Storms:** Magic rooms cost -20% but are unstable (random events)

### Reputation Discounts:

High reputation in categories grants build benefits:

- **High Wealth Reputation:** Gold-cost rooms -15%
- **High Knowledge Reputation:** Research unlocks cost -25% Research
- **High Terror Reputation:** Dark rooms cost -20%, but attracts more invasions
- **High Harmony Reputation:** All rooms -10%, but cannot build high-corruption rooms

## Room Balance Strategy

**Design Intent:**

1. **Early game (Days 1-30):** Limited selection forces experimentation with basic rooms
2. **Mid game (Days 31-100):** Unlock Tier 2, create specialized strategies
3. **Late game (Days 101+):** Tier 3 rooms enable powerful combinations but require significant investment

**Balance Levers:**

- **Cost scaling** prevents single room spam
- **Build limits** force diverse room types
- **Unlock gates** create progression curve
- **Floor restrictions** encourage vertical expansion
- **Biome restrictions** make each floor unique
- **Cooldowns** prevent rapid power spikes

**Player Choice Preservation:**

Despite limits, players can still pursue different strategies:
- **Undead army:** Prioritize Soul Wells (max 2) + many Barracks (unlimited)
- **Resource hoard:** Prioritize Mines (unlimited) + Vaults (limited) + Forges (limited)
- **Magic powerhouse:** Prioritize Nexus (limited) + Summoning (limited) + Alchemy (unlimited after research)
- **Breeding focus:** Prioritize Breeding Pits (max 2) + Spawning Pools (max 3) + Training (unlimited)

No strategy is "locked out," but each has different bottlenecks and strengths.

# Room Placement & Connection System

## Grid-Based Placement

**Dungeon Grid:**
- Each floor is a 20x20 grid of tiles
- Rooms occupy multiple tiles based on their tetromino shape
- Rooms CANNOT overlap or be rotated
- Must be placed on valid empty grid space

**Tetromino Shapes:**
- **Square 2x2:** Occupies a 2x2 area (4 tiles)
- **Square 3x3:** Occupies a 3x3 area (9 tiles)
- **Square 4x4:** Occupies a 4x4 area (16 tiles)
- **L-shaped:** Occupies 4 tiles in an L configuration (like Tetris L-piece)
- **T-shaped:** Occupies 4 tiles in a T configuration (like Tetris T-piece)
- **I-shaped (1x4):** Occupies 4 tiles in a straight line
- **T-shaped (3x3):** Occupies 7 tiles (3x3 with two corners missing)
- **T-shaped (3x4):** Occupies 9 tiles in a T configuration

## Connection Methods

Rooms connect to each other through **hallways** or **direct adjacency** (with restrictions).

### Hallway System

**Basic Hallways:**
- Cost: 5 Crystals per tile
- Connect any two rooms
- Can be any length, any path (horizontal, vertical, corners allowed)
- Occupy 1 tile width
- Inhabitants/defenders can be stationed in hallways
- Traps can be placed in hallways

**Hallway Mechanics:**
- **Maximum length:** 15 tiles (prevents sprawl, encourages compact design)
- **Minimum length:** 1 tile (direct neighbor connection)
- **Corner turns:** No cost penalty, but each turn counts toward trap placement
- **Pathfinding:** Invaders choose shortest valid path to objective
- **Visibility:** Player sees all connected rooms clearly

**Hallway Upgrades (Features):**
- **Reinforced Walls** (10 Crystals) - +1 Defense, slows invaders by 1 turn
- **Secret Passages** (15 Flux) - Hidden on invader map, confuses pathfinding
- **Pit Trap** (10 Crystals) - Damages/delays first invader passing through (one-time)
- **Arrow Trap** (15 Crystals) - Damages up to 3 invaders (reusable, 1/invasion)
- **Rune Ward** (10 Flux) - Damages magic users, prevents teleportation
- **Collapsing Ceiling** (20 Crystals) - Can manually collapse to seal off section (destroys hallway)
- **Patrol Route** (Feature) - Assigned defenders automatically patrol between connected rooms
- **Teleport Rune** (25 Flux) - Instant travel for your creatures, not invaders

**Hallway Strategy:**
- Long winding hallways = more trap opportunities, slower invasions
- Short direct hallways = faster resource movement, efficient layout
- Hub-and-spoke design = one central room with many hallway connections
- Maze design = confuse invaders, multiple paths to critical rooms
- Linear design = easy to defend chokepoints

### Direct Adjacency

**When Rooms Can Connect Directly (no hallway):**
- Two rooms share at least 1 edge tile on the grid
- Player must enable "direct connection" (free, instant)
- Creates a doorway between rooms

**Direct Adjacency Benefits:**
- No hallway cost (saves Crystals)
- Faster resource/inhabitant movement
- Triggers adjacency bonuses (see room descriptions)
- Compact design (space-efficient)

**Direct Adjacency Drawbacks:**
- Invaders move through quickly (no hallway traps)
- Fear/Corruption/effects spread more easily
- Less control over defensive layers
- Harder to isolate compromised sections

**Adjacency Bonuses:**
Many rooms grant bonuses to adjacent rooms (see individual room descriptions):
- Crystal Mine + Dark Forge = Material bonus
- Mushroom Grove + Underground Lake = Growth bonus
- Library + Summoning Circle = Research breakthrough
- Soul Well spreads Corruption to adjacent rooms

**Adjacency Requirements:**
- Rooms must touch on at least 1 edge tile
- Corner-to-corner touching does NOT count as adjacent
- Diagonal connections are NOT adjacency (must share edge)

### Connection Strategy Examples

**1. Defensive Fortress (Hallway-Heavy):**
```
[Entrance]--long hallway with traps-->[Barracks]-->[Chokepoint]
                                             |
                                    [short hallway]
                                             |
                                          [Altar]
```
- Maximizes trap effectiveness
- Creates defensive layers
- Slows invader advance
- High Crystal cost for hallways

**2. Efficient Production (Adjacency-Heavy):**
```
[Mine][Mine]
[Forge][Lake]
[Grove][Grove]
```
- All rooms touch, triggering adjacency bonuses
- No hallway costs
- Fast resource movement
- Vulnerable to invasion (no defensive depth)

**3. Hybrid Design (Mixed):**
```
[Mine]--hallway-->[Forge]<--hallway--[Barracks]
  |                                      ^
direct                                   |
  |                               long trapped hallway
  v                                      |
[Grove]==========[Altar]           [Entrance]
```
- Adjacency for production synergy
- Hallways for defense
- Balanced approach

**4. Hub Design:**
```
        [Room]
           |
    [Room]-[Altar]-[Room]
           |
        [Room]
```
- Central Altar connected to everything
- Easy resource management
- Single point of failure (if Altar invaded)

**5. Layered Defense:**
```
Floor 1: [Trap Rooms]--maze of hallways-->[Decoy Vaults]
Floor 2: [Barracks]=====[Production Rooms]
Floor 3: [Altar]=====[Real Vault]=====[Throne]
```
- Sacrificial first floor
- Protected core on deep floors
- Vertical separation for security

### Connection Rules & Restrictions

**Mandatory Connections:**
- Altar must connect to at least 2 other rooms (redundancy)
- All rooms must have a valid path to the Altar (no orphaned sections)
- Entrance point (Floor 1 edge) must have path to interior

**Optional Isolation:**
- Rooms CAN be isolated (no connections) but:
  - Cannot produce resources (no workers can reach)
  - Cannot be defended (no defenders can reach)
  - Useful for "sealed" rooms (e.g., hibernating Ancient Treant, dangerous experiments)

**Connection Limits:**
- Each room can have unlimited connections
- Hallways can branch (T-junctions, 4-way intersections)
- Interconnected networks are encouraged

**Visual Representation:**
- Connected rooms: Highlighted borders show connection points
- Hallways: Visible paths on grid, show trap placements
- Unconnected rooms: Greyed out, show isolation warning

### Movement Through Connections

**Inhabitant Movement:**
- Workers move instantly between connected rooms when reassigned
- Can work in any connected room
- Fungal Network feature allows teleport between specific rooms

**Resource Flow:**
- Resources auto-distribute through connections
- Shortest path algorithm
- Can set priority rooms (resources flow there first)

**Defender Movement:**
- During invasions, defenders can move 1 room per turn through connections
- Can reinforce adjacent rooms
- Patrol hallways automatically if assigned

**Invader Movement:**
- Follow shortest path to objective through hallways
- Triggered by traps in hallways
- Can choose alternate paths if blocked

### Connection Costs Summary

| Connection Type | Cost | Build Time | Benefits | Drawbacks |
|----------------|------|------------|----------|-----------|
| Direct Adjacency | Free | Instant | Adjacency bonuses, fast movement | No defensive depth |
| Basic Hallway (per tile) | 5 Crystals | 1 minute/tile | Trap placement, control | Costs add up |
| Reinforced Hallway | +10 Crystals | +2 minutes | Extra defense | Expensive |
| Secret Passage | 15 Flux total | 5 minutes | Confuses invaders | High magic cost |
| Vertical (Stairs) | 20 Crystals | 5 minutes | Floor connection | One floor only |
| Vertical (Elevator) | 50 Crystals, 20 Flux | 10 minutes | Multi-floor fast travel | High maintenance |
| Vertical (Portal) | 100 Flux, 30 Essence | 15 minutes | Instant travel, any floors | Corruption risk |

# Inhabitants

## Design Philosophy
- Each inhabitant has base traits + conditional modifiers
- No inhabitant is optimal for just one room
- Fear and hunger create dynamic decision-making
- Unique inhabitants provide dungeon-wide effects

## Inhabitant Role System

All inhabitants can fulfill multiple roles, but with varying effectiveness. There are no hard "classes" - instead, each creature has **role affinities** based on their traits and stats.

### The Three Core Roles

**1. Worker**
**2. Defender**
**3. Specialist**

Every inhabitant can be assigned to any role, but their effectiveness varies dramatically.

---

### Role 1: WORKER

**Definition:** Inhabitants stationed in production/utility rooms to generate resources.

**How It Works:**
- Assign inhabitant to any room
- They contribute to that room's base production
- Effectiveness = Base Production Ã— (1 + Worker Efficiency Bonus)
- Can be stationed in multiple rooms if creature has "multi-task" trait

**Worker Efficiency Calculation:**

```
Final Production = Room Base Production Ã— (1 + Trait Bonuses) Ã— (1 + Room Synergy) Ã— Fear Modifier Ã— Hunger Modifier
```

**Example:**
- Crystal Mine base: 5 Crystals/min
- Station Kobold (Miner +20%): 5 Ã— 1.20 = 6 Crystals/min
- If adjacent to Forge (Material Synergy +30%): 6 Ã— 1.30 = 7.8 Crystals/min
- If Kobold is scared (Stress-eater, +10%): 7.8 Ã— 1.10 = 8.58 Crystals/min
- If Kobold is hungry (Slogging, -50%): 8.58 Ã— 0.50 = 4.29 Crystals/min

**Best Workers By Room Type:**

| Room Type | Best Workers | Efficiency Bonus |
|-----------|-------------|------------------|
| Crystal Mine | Kobold, Stone Elemental | +20-40% |
| Mushroom Grove | Myconid, Dryad | +20-30% |
| Shadow Library | Lich, Wraith, Beholder | +20-40% |
| Soul Well | Lich, Wraith | +30% spawn rate |
| Dark Forge | Stone Elemental, Orc, Goblin | +20-30% |
| Alchemy Lab | Goblin, Imp | +20% |
| Training Grounds | Orc, Gargoyle | +30% training speed |
| Summoning Circle | Lich, Demon Lord | -20% summon cost |
| Treasure Vault | Mimic, Dragon | +50% gold generation |
| Underground Lake | Slime, Myconid | +25% |

**Workers That Work Anywhere:**
- **Slime:** 70% base efficiency in ANY room (versatile)
- **Mimic:** 80% base efficiency in ANY room (shapeshifter)
- **Skeleton:** 90% base efficiency (tireless), but +1 Fear to room

### Role 2: DEFENDER

**Definition:** Inhabitants assigned to defensive positions to fight invaders.

**How It Works:**
- Assign inhabitant to Barracks, hallway, or defensive position in room
- They fight when invaders enter their zone
- Combat effectiveness = Base Combat Power + Combat Bonuses
- Can patrol between connected rooms if assigned to hallway

**Combat Stats (Every Inhabitant Has These):**

```
Combat Power = (HP Ã— Attack) / (Defense Factor)
```

- **HP (Hit Points):** How much damage they can take (10-100)
- **Attack:** Damage dealt per turn (1-20)
- **Defense:** Damage reduction (0-10)
- **Speed:** Initiative/turn order (1-10)
- **Special Abilities:** Unique combat effects

**Example Combat Stats:**

| Creature | HP | Attack | Defense | Speed | Combat Power |
|----------|-----|--------|---------|-------|--------------|
| Goblin | 15 | 3 | 1 | 6 | 45 (weak) |
| Kobold | 12 | 2 | 1 | 7 | 24 (very weak) |
| Skeleton | 20 | 5 | 3 | 4 | 100 (average) |
| Myconid | 10 | 1 | 0 | 2 | 10 (pitiful) |
| Orc | 40 | 10 | 4 | 5 | 400 (strong) |
| Gargoyle | 60 | 8 | 7 | 3 | 480 (very strong) |
| Wraith | 25 | 12 | 2 | 8 | 300 (strong, fast) |
| Lich | 50 | 15 | 5 | 6 | 750 (very strong) |
| Dragon | 200 | 30 | 15 | 4 | 6000 (legendary) |
| Medusa | 80 | 25 | 8 | 7 | 2000 (legendary) |

**Combat Modifiers:**

- **Fear Bonus:** If stationed in High Fear room, defenders get +2 Attack (invaders hesitate)
- **Terrain Bonus:** Hallway defenders get +20% damage (ambush advantage)
- **Numbers Advantage:** For each allied defender in same room, +5% damage (stacks, max 5 allies)
- **Equipment Bonus:** If Dark Forge has crafted equipment, +2 Attack, +1 Defense
- **Training Bonus:** If trained in Training Grounds, +1 Attack, +10% HP (permanent, max 3 stacks)
- **Fear Immunity:** Creatures with Fearless trait ignore enemy fear effects
- **Special Abilities:** See individual creature descriptions

**Defender Effectiveness Tiers:**

- **Poor Defenders (<50 Combat Power):** Goblin, Kobold, Myconid, Slime, Imp
  - These should primarily be workers, only fight as last resort
  
- **Average Defenders (50-200 Combat Power):** Skeleton, Stone Elemental, Dryad
  - Can hold defensive positions, but not frontline troops
  
- **Good Defenders (200-500 Combat Power):** Orc, Gargoyle, Wraith, Mimic
  - Primary defensive force, station in Barracks
  
- **Elite Defenders (500-1500 Combat Power):** Lich, Beholder, Demon Lord
  - Powerful guardians, but expensive to maintain
  
- **Legendary Defenders (1500+ Combat Power):** Dragon, Medusa, Ancient Treant
  - Game-changing defenders, can hold entire floors alone

**Defender Assignment Strategy:**

**Option A: Dedicated Defenders**
- Station combat-focused creatures in Barracks only
- They don't produce resources, but always ready for combat
- Best for: Orc, Gargoyle, trained Skeletons

**Option B: Dual-Role Workers/Defenders**
- Station creatures in production rooms near defensive zones
- During invasion, they can be "called to arms" (takes 1 turn to move)
- Best for: Skeleton (works at night, defends during day invasions)

**Option C: Patrol Assignments**
- Assign creatures to hallways
- They patrol between connected rooms
- Automatically engage invaders in patrol zone
- Best for: Wraith (intangible, can patrol anywhere), Gargoyle (stone form when idle)

**Option D: Room Guards**
- Station powerful defenders IN critical rooms (Altar, Vault, Throne)
- They work AND defend simultaneously (but at -20% efficiency for both)
- Best for: Mimic (in Vault), Dragon (in Throne), Beholder (in Library)

### Role 3: SPECIALIST

**Definition:** Inhabitants with unique abilities that trigger under specific conditions.

**How It Works:**
- Specialists aren't assigned specifically - their abilities are PASSIVE or CONDITIONAL
- They activate automatically when conditions are met
- Can stack with Worker or Defender role

**Specialist Types:**

**A. Fusion Catalyst:**
- Required for specific creature fusions
- Example: Lich required to fuse Wraith + Skeleton â†’ Death Knight

**B. Research Enabler:**
- Unlocks special research trees
- Example: Station Lich in Library â†’ Unlock Lichdom research
- Example: Station Demon Lord in Summoning Circle â†’ Unlock Demonic Pacts

**C. Dungeon-Wide Aura:**
- Provides passive bonuses to entire dungeon (or entire floor)
- Example: Dragon in Throne â†’ +200% Gold generation dungeon-wide
- Example: Ancient Treant â†’ +100% Food production dungeon-wide

**D. Event Trigger:**
- Enables special events or mechanics

- Example: Beholder reveals hidden features across dungeon

**E. Resource Converter:**
- Can transform one resource into another
- Example: Slime consumes waste, gains traits from what it eats
- Example: Imp causes random events (can produce any resource randomly)

**F. Fear Manipulator:**
- Changes fear levels in rooms
- Example: Skeleton generates +1 Fear wherever stationed
- Example: Dryad reduces Fear by -2 in stationed room + adjacent rooms
- Example: Medusa generates +3 Fear in stationed room + adjacent rooms

**G. Corruption Manipulator:**
- Generates or cleanses Corruption
- Example: Demon Lord generates +10 Corruption/min
- Example: Ancient Treant cleanses -10 Corruption/min
- Example: Dryad cleanses -2 Corruption/min in stationed room

**Specialists in Practice:**

**Example 1: The Lich**

## Inhabitants as Defenders: Combat Details

**Combat Turn Flow:**

1. **Initiative Phase:** Defenders and invaders roll Speed check
2. **Attack Phase:** Highest speed goes first, rolls Attack vs Enemy Defense
3. **Damage Phase:** If hit, deal (Attack - Defense) damage to enemy HP
4. **Special Ability Phase:** Some creatures trigger abilities (Medusa petrify, Wraith fear)
5. **Repeat until one side is defeated or retreats**

**Special Combat Abilities:**

- **Skeleton:** Fearless (immune to fear), Reassemble (25% chance to revive after death)
- **Orc:** Berserker Rage (when damaged below 50% HP, +100% attack, -2 defense)
- **Wraith:** Intangible (50% chance to evade physical attacks)
- **Gargoyle:** Stone Form (when idle, +100% defense, cannot attack)
- **Medusa:** Petrifying Gaze (10% chance per turn to permanently petrify invader)
- **Dragon:** Breath Weapon (hits all enemies in room, massive damage, 3-turn cooldown)
- **Lich:** Spell Master (can cast 3 spells: Death Bolt, Raise Dead, Shield)
- **Demon Lord:** Dark Pact (sacrifice Corruption to summon lesser demons mid-combat)
- **Beholder:** Anti-Magic (disables enemy magic, eye rays deal magic damage)

**Combat Example:**

```
INVASION: 3 Warriors enter Barracks

Defenders:
  - Orc (40 HP, 10 Attack, 4 Defense, Speed 5)
  - Skeleton (20 HP, 5 Attack, 3 Defense, Speed 4)

  - Warrior (30 HP, 8 Attack, 5 Defense, Speed 3)

  - Orc attacks Warrior 1: (10 - 5) = 5 damage â†’ Warrior 1 at 25 HP

  - Skeleton 2 attacks Warrior 2: (5 - 5) = 0 damage â†’ Miss!
  - Warrior 1 attacks Orc: (8 - 4) = 4 damage â†’ Orc at 36 HP
  - Warrior 2 attacks Skeleton 1: (8 - 3) = 5 damage â†’ Skeleton 1 at 15 HP
  - Warrior 3 attacks Skeleton 2: (8 - 3) = 5 damage â†’ Skeleton 2 at 15 HP

  - Orc attacks Warrior 1: (10 - 5) = 5 damage â†’ Warrior 1 at 20 HP
  - Skeleton 1 attacks Warrior 1: (5 - 5) = 0 damage â†’ Miss!
  - Skeleton 2 attacks Warrior 2: (5 - 5) = 0 damage â†’ Miss!
```

**Defender Lessons:**
- Skeletons are weak individually, strong in numbers
- Orcs excel against armored foes
- Need variety: high-attack creatures + tanky creatures + support

## Summary: Can Any Creature Be Anything?

**YES, but with massive effectiveness differences:**

- **Any creature can work** â†’ Some are 10x better than others in specific rooms
- **Any creature can defend** â†’ Combat power varies from 10 to 6000
- **Some creatures are specialists** â†’ Unique abilities that transcend roles

**Design ensures:**
1. No "useless" creatures (even Myconid is best farmer)
2. No "does everything" creatures (even Dragon needs 50 Gold/day upkeep)
3. Strategic decisions matter (assign Orc to Barracks or Forge?)
4. Dungeon composition matters (5 Skeletons > 1 Skeleton)
5. Room placement matters (Defenders need to reach invaded rooms)

Players must balance:
- **Production needs** (workers in resource rooms)
- **Defense needs** (defenders in strategic positions)
- **Specialist bonuses** (getting unique creature benefits)
- **Upkeep costs** (feeding/maintaining inhabitants)

# Floor System

## Floor Mechanics

Dungeons can be built across multiple floors (vertical expansion).

### Floor 1 (Surface Level)
- **Bonuses:** Easy access for recruitment, +20% food production (sunlight)
- **Penalties:** More vulnerable to invasions, -10% Corruption generation
- **Special:** Can build surface structures (gardens, watchtowers)

### Floors 2-3 (Upper Depths)
- **Bonuses:** Balanced environment, standard production
- **Penalties:** None
- **Special:** Best for housing and general purpose rooms

### Floors 4-6 (Deep Depths)
- **Bonuses:** +10% Crystal/Gold mining per floor, +5% Corruption/floor
- **Penalties:** -15% Food production, +1 Fear level baseline
- **Special:** Geothermal vents available, rare ore veins

### Floors 7-9 (Abyssal Depths)
- **Bonuses:** +20% Crystal/Gold per floor, +10% Corruption/floor, rare creatures spawn
- **Penalties:** -30% Food production, +2 Fear levels, hard to access
- **Special:** Can encounter underground civilizations, Mithril veins

### Floor 10+ (Primordial Abyss)
- **Bonuses:** +50% all rare resources, unique creatures available
- **Penalties:** -50% Food, +3 Fear, extreme danger
- **Special:** Ancient artifacts, world bosses, ultimate challenges

## Invasion System

Periodically, heroes/adventurers invade to "cleanse" your dungeon.

### Invasion Frequency

**Base Invasion Schedule:**
- **Days 1-30:** No invasions (grace period)
- **Days 31-90:** 1 invasion every 10-15 days
- **Days 91-180:** 1 invasion every 7-10 days
- **Days 181+:** 1 invasion every 5-7 days

**Frequency Modifiers:**
- High Terror reputation: +1 invasion per period
- High Wealth (>1000 Gold): +1 invasion per period
- Successful defense streak: Next invasion delayed by 2 days
- Failed defense: Next invasion accelerated by 3 days
- Certain events or research can trigger immediate invasions

### Invasion Entry Points

**Entry Point Selection (Roll d100):**
1. **Surface Entrance (40%)** - Floor 1, nearest room to "entrance" (opposite side from Altar)
2. **Side Tunnel (25%)** - Random floor 1-3, random edge room
3. **Collapsed Ceiling (15%)** - Random floor 2-4, random room (surprise!)
4. **Ancient Portal (10%)** - If Summoning Circle exists, starts there
5. **Sewer Access (10%)** - If Underground Lake exists, starts there

**Player Can Influence Entry:**
- Build Watchtower (Floor 1 feature): Prevents surprise entries, always Surface Entrance
- Seal Portals (Summoning Circle upgrade): Prevents Portal entries
- Drain Lake (Underground Lake toggle): Prevents Sewer entries

### Invasion Composition

**Invader Party Size:**
- Base: 3-5 invaders
- +1 per 10 rooms in dungeon
- +1 per 500 Gold in treasury
- +2 if High Terror reputation
- Maximum: 15 invaders

**Invader Types (based on dungeon profile):**
- **High Corruption:** Paladins, Clerics, Holy Knights (anti-undead)
- **High Wealth:** Thieves, Rogues, Treasure Hunters (fast, sneaky)
- **High Knowledge:** Wizards, Arcane Inquisitors (anti-magic)
- **Balanced:** Mixed adventuring party
- **Random Events:** Rival dungeon lords, demon hunters, monster slayers

**Invader Classes:**
- **Warriors:** High HP, slow, bash through traps
- **Rogues:** Low HP, fast, disarm traps, steal resources
- **Mages:** Medium HP, ranged attacks, destroy magical features
- **Clerics:** Medium HP, heal allies, extra damage vs undead
- **Rangers:** Medium HP, track paths, bonus vs beasts
- **Paladins:** High HP, high damage, fear immunity, anti-corruption

### Invasion Mechanics (Turn-Based)

**Movement Phase (Invaders):**
1. Invaders move 1 room per turn
2. They pathfind toward the Altar (primary objective)
3. Will detour for high-value targets (Treasure Vault, Throne Room, Soul Well)
4. Avoid high-fear rooms if possible (morale check)

**Hallway Encounters:**
- Invaders trigger ALL traps in hallway (dice roll to avoid/disarm)
- Trapped hallways slow movement by 1 turn
- Stationed guards in hallways fight as ambushes (+20% damage)

**Room Encounters:**
When invaders enter a room:

1. **Check for defenders:**
   - Count all inhabitants stationed in room
   - Count defenders from adjacent Barracks (if connected)
   - Roll initiative (invaders usually go first unless ambushed)

2. **Combat Resolution:**
   - Each combatant rolls attack (d20 + combat stat)
   - Damage dealt on hit (varies by class/creature)
   - Defenders fight until room is "cleared" or invaders defeated
   - Invaders fight until victorious or retreat (morale check)

3. **Room Victory (Invaders):**
   - Defeat all defenders OR defenders flee
   - Spend 1 turn "sacking" room (steal resources, damage features)
   - **Room Damage:** 20-40% production penalty until repaired (costs 50% original build cost)
   - **Resource Loss:** Lose 20% of stored resources in that room type
   - **Inhabitant Fate:** Killed (permanent), Captured (can rescue/ransom), or Fled (returns after invasion)

4. **Room Victory (Defenders):**
   - Defeat all invaders in room
   - Gain experience for stationed defenders
   - Loot invader equipment (Gold, items)
   - Prisoners can be captured (send to Torture Chamber or Spawning Pool)

**Fear Effects on Invaders:**
- **Low Fear Room:** No effect
- **Medium Fear Room:** Morale check (10% chance to flee)
- **High Fear Room:** Morale check (30% chance to flee), -2 to attack rolls
- **Very High Fear Room:** Morale check (50% chance to flee), -4 to attack rolls, some invaders may freeze in terror

**Morale System:**
- Invaders start with 100 morale
- Lose 10 morale per ally defeated
- Lose 5 morale per trap triggered
- Lose 15 morale in High/Very High fear rooms
- Gain 10 morale per room captured
- At 0 morale: Invaders retreat from dungeon

### Invasion Objectives

**Primary Objective (Always):**
- Reach and destroy/desecrate the Altar
- If successful: Lose 1 civilization level, must rebuild Altar (100 Crystals, 50 Gold)

**Secondary Objectives (1-2 randomly selected):**
1. **Slay the Monster:** Kill specific legendary creature (Dragon, Lich, Demon Lord)
2. **Rescue Prisoner:** Free captured invader from previous raid (if any in Torture Chamber)
3. **Steal Treasure:** Reach Treasure Vault, steal 50% of Gold
4. **Seal the Portal:** Destroy Summoning Circle
5. **Purge the Darkness:** Destroy Soul Well or highest Corruption room
6. **Recover Artifact:** Steal specific feature or item from Throne Room

**Victory Conditions:**

*Invaders Win if:*
- They complete primary objective (reach Altar)
- OR complete 2 secondary objectives
- OR player surrenders

*Defenders Win if:*
- All invaders are killed
- OR all invaders retreat (morale = 0)
- OR invaders fail to complete primary objective within 30 turns

### Defense Strategies:

1. **Trap Maze:** 
   - Wind hallways into long paths
   - Layer multiple trap types
   - Force invaders through High Fear zones
   - Slow their advance, wear down morale

2. **Monster Army:** 
   - Station combat creatures in Barracks near entry points
   - Create "kill zones" with concentrated defenders
   - Use Gargoyles/Skeletons for 24/7 defense
   - Coordinate with Training Grounds for elite units

3. **Fear Tactics:** 
   - Create high-fear corridors leading to Altar
   - Station Medusa, Wraiths, or Demons in key rooms
   - Break invader morale before combat
   - Force retreats without losing defenders

4. **Magical Defense:** 
   - Use Summoning Circle to spawn temporary defenders
   - Mages in Ley Line Nexus gain massive power
   - Trap Glyphs trigger automatic spells
   - Beholder provides magical counter-attacks

5. **Fortress Design:** 
   - Centralize critical rooms (Altar, Vault, Throne)
   - Create multiple defensive layers (Floor 1 = sacrificial, Floor 2-3 = main defense)
   - Chokepoints where 1 strong defender can hold many invaders
   - Dead-end decoy rooms to waste invader turns

6. **Resource Denial:**
   - Move Gold to deep vaults before invasion (if warned)
   - Station Mimics to ambush treasure hunters
   - Sacrifice outer rooms to protect core
   - Use Torture Chamber to convert captured invaders to defenders

### Invasion Rewards:

**Successful Defense:**
- **Reputation Bonus:** +10 Terror (if brutal), +10 Defense (if efficient)
- **Looted Equipment:** 5-15 Gold per defeated invader, random crafted items
- **Prisoners:** 1d4 captured invaders (can convert, sacrifice, or ransom)
- **Experience:** Defending creatures gain +10% combat effectiveness (permanent, max 3 stacks)
- **Morale Boost:** All inhabitants +10% production for 3 days

**Captured Prisoners (per prisoner):**
- **Execute:** Gain 5 Corruption, +Terror reputation
- **Ransom:** Gain 20-50 Gold (varies by prisoner class)
- **Convert:** Spend 3 days in Torture Chamber, 30% chance to join as neutral inhabitant
- **Sacrifice:** Use in Soul Well for +2 Essence
- **Experiment:** Use in Breeding Pits for hybrid research

**Failed Defense (Partial or Total):**
- **Room Damage:** Repair costs for sacked rooms
- **Resource Loss:** Lost Gold/Crystals/Food from captured rooms
- **Inhabitant Loss:** Killed defenders don't return (unless Phylactery exists)
- **Reputation Loss:** -20 Terror if easily defeated
- **Invasion Acceleration:** Next invasion comes 3 days sooner
- **Objective Loss:** If Altar destroyed, lose 1 civ level (rebuild required)

### Special Invasion Events:

**Heroic Invasion (Rare):**
- Famous hero leads party (Legendary combat stats)
- Party has special abilities (magic items, divine blessings)
- Defeating hero grants unique reward (legendary equipment, permanent bonus)
- If hero escapes, they'll return with reinforcements

**Rival Dungeon Lord (Very Rare):**
- Not trying to destroy, trying to steal creatures/features
- Uses mind control rather than killing
- Can negotiate/bribe to leave
- Defeating them grants access to their research

**Crusade (Crisis Event):**
- Triggered by 200+ Corruption or major evil act
- Massive invasion force (20-30 invaders)
- Multiple entry points simultaneously
- Extremely difficult, but massive rewards for survival
- Can be avoided by reducing Corruption or paying tribute (100 Gold)

**Inspector Visit (Bureaucratic Event):**
- Not hostile, just investigating
- Evaluates dungeon "safety standards"
- Can be bribed, charmed, or scared away
- If they report negatively, triggers future invasion
- If impressed, grants positive reputation

# Synergy Examples (Multi-layered)

## Example 1: The Undead Factory
**Rooms:** Soul Well + Library + Barracks + Torture Chamber  
**Inhabitants:** Lich (Library) + Skeletons (Soul Well) + Wraith (Torture Chamber)  
**Features:** Coffins in all rooms, Corruption Seal in Library  
**Result:**
- Soul Well produces skeletons enhanced by Lich's presence
- Library researches necromancy safely (Corruption Seal)
- Torture Chamber converts prisoners to undead
- Barracks houses undead army
- High synergy, but vulnerable to holy invasions

## Example 2: The Mushroom Kingdom
**Rooms:** Multiple Mushroom Groves + Underground Lake + Alchemy Lab  
**Inhabitants:** Myconids (Groves) + Dryad (Lake) + Slime (Alchemy)  
**Features:** Fungal Network connecting all groves, Bioluminescent Moss  
**Result:**
- Lake provides humidity bonus to all groves
- Myconids share production via Spore Network + Fungal Network
- Excess food converts to potions at Alchemy Lab
- Dryad reduces fear and boosts production
- Low fear, high food, sustainable

## Example 3: The Arcane Nexus
**Rooms:** Ley Line Nexus + Summoning Circle + Library + Alchemy Lab  
**Inhabitants:** Lich (Library) + Wraith (Nexus) + Beholder (Summoning)  
**Features:** Arcane Crystals in all rooms, Void Gate in Summoning Circle  
**Result:**
- Ley Line amplifies all magic rooms
- Lich researches with bonus from magic environment
- Summoning Circle gets free summons + Void Gate randomness
- Beholder provides vision and magical defense
- High Flux generation, powerful but unstable

## Example 4: The Mining Consortium
**Rooms:** Crystal Mines + Dark Forge + Treasure Vault  
**Inhabitants:** Kobolds (Mines) + Stone Elemental (Forge) + Mimic (Vault)  
**Features:** Geothermal Vents (Forge), Storage Expansion (Vault)  
**Result:**
- Mines provide raw materials to adjacent Forge
- Forge crafts equipment enhanced by Geothermal Vents
- Vault stores wealth, Mimic defends it
- Stone Elemental boosts mining and forging
- High crystal and gold generation

# Anti-Optimization Mechanics Summary

1. **Fear Cascades:** High-fear creatures are powerful but disrupt neighbors
2. **Resource Competition:** Multiple currencies needed simultaneously
3. **Conditional Bonuses:** "Best" inhabitant changes with context
4. **Adjacency Puzzles:** Room placement matters but optimal layout varies
5. **Day/Night Shifts:** Different creatures excel at different times
6. **Seasonal Variance:** Optimal strategy changes every 7 days
7. **Floor Depth Trade-offs:** Deeper = more resources but harder to manage
8. **Corruption Double-Edge:** Power comes with risks
9. **Fusion Costs:** Combining creatures is expensive, requires planning
10. **Invasion Scaling:** Success brings harder challenges
11. **Reputation Conflicts:** Can't maximize all reputation types
12. **Biome Restrictions:** Some strategies impossible in some biomes

# Final Notes

This design creates multiple viable strategies:
- **Undead horde** (Corruption focus)
- **Wealthy merchant dungeon** (Gold focus)
- **Research institute** (Knowledge focus)
- **Peaceful commune** (Harmony focus)
- **Chaotic laboratory** (Experimental focus)

No single approach dominates because:
- Each has unique challenges
- Resources compete for investment
- Reputation affects available options
- Environmental factors shift viability
- Creatures have conditional synergies, not absolute bests

The game rewards creativity and adaptation over optimization.
